Global CO2 Emissions
Climate Change Visualization through CO2 (1960-2022)
This visualization explains the global rise in carbon dioxide emissions since 1960. This explores the different growth trends and other major contributors and allows the user to extrapolate about the future of CO2 emissions.
Overall Global Trend
Since 1960, global CO2 emissions have grown rapidly. This chart shows the total emissions globally, revealing a dangerous trend putting our climate at risk.
Vizualization Takeaway: Global CO2 emissions have shown a positive growth rate since 1960, growing from approximately 9 billion tons to over 36 billion tons in 2022. The key points (highlighted in the chart) reveal a dramatic acceleration occuring after 1980- often attributed to the industrialization of China and other developing nations.
The Major Players
Large industialized countries contribute more to the global CO2 emissions relative to other nations. This ranking shows the world's largest CO2 emitters in 2022, revealing who truly needs to own responsibility for climate change.
Power Law Distribution: Just 5 countries (China, USA, India, Russia, Japan) account for ~60% of global CO2 emissions. China, by themselves, produces nearly 31% of the world's total.
